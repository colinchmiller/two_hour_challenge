Snake Plant

The Snake Plant (Sansevieria trifasciata) is also known as mother-in-law's tongue in the United States because of its sharp-edged leaves. Native to Western Africa, it is an understory plant and a survivalist. It can thrive in low-light conditions with infrequent watering. It is a superior air-cleaner, removing chemicals such as Trichloroethylene, Benzene, and Formaldehyde.
