Boston Fern

The Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata) is a wide ranging fern, common to forests and swamps throughout Central and South America as well as the West Indies and Africa. It is dependent upon humid conditions, which can make it more difficult to grow as an indoor plant during cold, dry, winter months. It benefits from regular misting and lower light conditions, often growing favorably indoors in bathrooms, where it can benefit from shower steam. It can also improve the air quality in bathrooms by removing chemicals from cleaning products or the byproducts of human excretion.
